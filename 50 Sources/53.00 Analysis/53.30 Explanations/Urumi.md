# ~={red}Definition=~

An **urumi** is a flexible bladed weapon originating from South India, characterized by one or more thin, whip-like [[Wootz steel|steel]] blades attached to a hilt and used through continuous, flowing motion.

---
# ~={red}Context=~
## ~={light-red}Background=~
The urumi developed in the Indian subcontinent, particularly in regions such as Kerala and Tamil Nadu, and is closely associated with martial traditions like **Kalaripayattu**. Its design reflects advanced metallurgical skill and a deep understanding of momentum-based combat.

## ~={light-red}Purpose=~
The weapon exists to maximize reach, unpredictability, and area control in combat, allowing a skilled user to strike multiple angles while maintaining distance from opponents.

---
# ~={red}Structure=~
## ~={light-red}Core Elements=~
- Flexible steel blade (single or multiple)  
- Central hilt, often similar to a sword grip  
- Optional hand guard  
- Coiled or belt-worn storage form  

## ~={light-red}Terminology=~
- **Flexible Blade** — Thin steel capable of bending without breaking  
- **Kalaripayattu** — Traditional South Indian martial art  
- **Momentum Weapon** — Weapon relying on continuous motion  
- **Whip Sword** — Common descriptive term for the urumi  

---
# ~={red}Function=~
## ~={light-red}Operation=~
The urumi is wielded using circular and figure-eight motions, keeping the blade in constant movement to generate tension and prevent it from collapsing or striking the wielder.

## ~={light-red}Process=~
1. Initiation of rotational movement  
2. Maintenance of blade tension through momentum  
3. Directional control via wrist and arm motion  
4. Striking, entangling, or deterrence through reach  
5. Continuous motion to avoid self-injury

---
# ~={red}Development=~

Over time, the urumi evolved into multiple regional variants, including multi-bladed forms. In modern contexts, its use is largely confined to martial arts practice and cultural exhibitions rather than active combat.

---
# ~={red}Notes=~

The urumi is considered one of the most difficult traditional weapons to master due to the high risk of self-injury. Effective use requires precise timing, spatial awareness, and sustained motion.
