# ~={red}Definition=~

Johnny.Decimal is a hierarchical numbering and naming system used for organizing digital information by assigning structured numeric identifiers to categories and items.

---
# ~={red}Context=~
## ~={light-red}Background=~
Johnny.Decimal was created by John “Johnny” Noble as a response to the limitations of traditional folder-based organization systems. It draws inspiration from classification schemes such as library decimal systems and accounting charts of accounts.

## ~={light-red}Purpose=~
The system exists to provide a scalable, unambiguous method for organizing files, notes, and projects in a way that remains stable over time and reduces cognitive load.

---
# ~={red}Structure=~
## ~={light-red}Core Elements=~
- Numeric area ranges (e.g., 10–19, 20–29)  
- Categories within each area  
- Individual items identified by decimals  
- Fixed, pre-planned numbering scheme  

## ~={light-red}Terminology=~
- **Area** — High-level numeric range representing a domain  
- **Category** — Subdivision within an area  
- **ID** — Numeric identifier assigned to an item  
- **Index** — Reference document describing the system  

---
# ~={red}Function=~
## ~={light-red}Operation=~
Information is assigned a numeric identifier based on its area and category, allowing users to locate, reference, and store items consistently across different tools and platforms.

## ~={light-red}Process=~
1. Define high-level areas and assign numeric ranges  
2. Create categories within each area  
3. Assign decimal identifiers to individual items  
4. Store items using their numeric IDs and names  
5. Reference items by ID in notes and workflows  

---
# ~={red}Applications=~
## ~={light-red}Use Cases=~
- Personal knowledge management systems  
- File and folder organization  
- Project and documentation management  
- Cross-tool information referencing  

## ~={light-red}Examples=~
- Obsidian vault organization  
- Digital document archives  
- Note-taking systems using numeric prefixes  

---
# ~={red}Development=~

The Johnny.Decimal system has evolved through community feedback and practical use, expanding from a personal workflow into a widely adopted organizational methodology with documented guidelines and examples.

# ~={red}Notes=~

Johnny.Decimal emphasizes stability over constant restructuring; once numeric ranges are defined, they should change rarely. The system is tool-agnostic and can be applied to both digital and physical organization.
